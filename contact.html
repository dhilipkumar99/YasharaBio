Media & Press

Yashara does not have a communications department in the conventional sense — we are a small scientific company and our media contact is a person, not a PR firm. If you are a journalist, science writer, podcast host, or editor covering life sciences software, laboratory automation, or computational biology, we are genuinely happy to talk. We can speak to the product, the science behind it, and the institutional problem each addresses. We ask only that you identify your outlet and the nature of your coverage in your first message so we can be useful to you.

FluorocellAI Team

The scientists who built FluorocellAI answer this address directly. If you are evaluating cell segmentation software for a specific imaging modality — whether that is widefield fluorescence, spinning disk confocal, lightsheet, or a plate-based HCS system — and want to understand concretely how FluorocellAI handles your cell type, your staining protocol, or your throughput requirements, write here. Demonstrations are not scripted tours; they are technical conversations. If you tell us your cell line and imaging setup beforehand, we will come prepared with relevant examples rather than generic ones.

fluorocell@yashara.us

ComplianceCall Team

Clinical laboratory compliance is not a generic domain, and ComplianceCall was not built generically. This team handles everything from first-contact questions about what the platform covers and how it maps to your certificate type, through to active onboarding, module configuration, and the execution of Business Associate Agreements for HIPAA-covered environments. If you are a laboratory director, technical consultant, or quality manager trying to understand whether ComplianceCall fits your specific regulatory situation — including multi-site operations, specialty laboratory categories, or state-level survey preparation — this is the right place to start that conversation.

compliancecall_admin@yashara.us

PuroChem Team

PuroChem exists because GHS hazard classification is not a static fact — it is a moving target, and most databases treat it as though it were not. Alex leads the PuroChem data and methodology team. If you have a question about how we source and validate classification data, how we handle discrepancies between jurisdictional implementations of GHS, how band-level reclassification events are detected and recorded, or whether a specific chemical or chemical class is within our current coverage scope, write directly here. We also field questions from EHS professionals and procurement teams about workflow integration, and from researchers curious about the underlying toxicological evidence we use to contextualize classification changes.

alex_pchem@yashara.us

What Happens Next

We read every inquiry personally.

Your inquiry goes directly to a member of our scientific staff — not a sales development representative following a script. We respond within two business days with a substantive reply tailored to your specific question or context, not a boilerplate introduction email.

Demonstrations

Tailored to your workflow, not a generic tour.

Product demonstrations are conducted by the scientists who built the product. We ask for a brief description of your laboratory context and the specific problem you are trying to solve so we can demonstrate the relevant capabilities using examples meaningful to your work — not the same deck we show everyone.

Evaluation Access

Structured 30-day trial access.

For qualified research teams and clinical laboratories, we provide structured 30-day evaluation access configured to your specific context. For FluorocellAI, this means we configure the evaluation environment with example data representative of your cell type and imaging modality where possible. No credit card required for evaluation.

Yashara Biosciences, Inc. processes personal information submitted through this form solely for the purpose of responding to your inquiry. We do not sell, share, or license personal data to third parties for marketing purposes. For a complete description of our data handling practices, please refer to our Privacy Policy.
